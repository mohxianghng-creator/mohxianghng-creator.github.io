About Me

I am an actuarial analyst with a strong interest in data science, financial systems, and AI. I focus on modeling extreme uncertainty in insurance and financial systems, particularly in catastrophe risk, capital modeling, and climate-driven financial losses. I develop simulation-based and data-driven models using Python, statistical methods, and geospatial analytics to study real-world insurance risk structures.

Projects

Interest Rate & Inflation Analysis

Analyzing how central bank policies influence inflation and economic behavior.

Liquidity & Banking System Model

Exploring how banks manage liquidity under stress conditions.

Dynamic Catastrophe Risk Simulator

Multi-hazard catastrophe model simulating earthquakes, floods, and hurricanes using Extreme Value Theory (EVT), t-Copula dependency modeling, Monte Carlo simulation, and insurance loss layering with geospatial visualization and scenario-based risk analysis.

Reinsurance Pricing & Structuring Engine

Pricing framework for proportional and non-proportional reinsurance treaties (quota share, surplus, excess-of-loss), evaluating expected loss, risk transfer efficiency, and capital relief under different treaty structures.

Capital Adequacy & ERM Simulation Model

Stochastic capital model simulating insurer balance sheet risk using Monte Carlo methods, including SCR estimation, stress testing, and economic capital allocation under extreme scenarios.

Climate & Natural Catastrophe Risk Analytics

Climate risk framework linking historical catastrophe data with future climate scenarios (2030–2100) to assess long-term insurance loss impact under changing hazard conditions.
