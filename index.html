About

I am an actuarial science student focused on risk analytics, insurance modeling, and financial systems.

My work focuses on understanding extreme uncertainty in insurance and financial systems, particularly in catastrophe risk, reinsurance pricing, capital modeling, and climate risk.

I build simulation-based and data-driven models using Python, statistical methods, and geospatial analytics to study real-world insurance risks.

Featured Projects

Dynamic Catastrophe Risk Simulator

Multi-hazard catastrophe model simulating earthquakes, floods, and hurricanes using Extreme Value Theory (EVT), t-Copula dependency modeling, Monte Carlo simulation, and insurance loss layering with geospatial visualization and scenario analysis.

Reinsurance Pricing & Structuring Engine

Pricing model for proportional and non-proportional reinsurance treaties including quota share, surplus, and excess-of-loss structures, evaluating expected loss, risk transfer efficiency, and capital relief.

Capital & ERM Simulation Model

Stochastic capital model simulating insurer balance sheet risk using Monte Carlo methods, including SCR estimation, stress testing, and economic capital allocation under extreme scenarios.

Climate Risk & Natural Catastrophe Analytics

Climate risk framework linking historical catastrophe data with future climate scenarios (2030–2100) to assess long-term insurance loss impact under changing hazard conditions.
